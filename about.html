about

the story behind tortoise

my name is deva.

i lived in tallinn for a few years. that city taught me a lot about myself. the quiet forests, the long winters, the feeling of walking home alone in the snow. it was beautiful and lonely in ways i didn't understand then.

but in tallinn, i was also living through something i didn't recognize at first. an abusive relationship that i thought was normal. i thought love was supposed to hurt. i thought staying was strength. i thought if i just tried harder, things would change.

i didn't see it clearly. not until much later.

🍒 🐢 🍒

then came a trip to florence.

i was traveling, trying to escape, trying to breathe. and somewhere in that beautiful city, i met a stranger. a beautiful stranger who said something i'll never forget.

"love isn't supposed to make you small.
it's supposed to make you feel like you can breathe."

it was just a moment. a conversation. a few words from someone i never saw again. but it cracked something open in me.

i started to realize that love is about feeling safe. feeling seen. feeling like you don't have to pretend. it's not about surviving someone. it's about coming home to yourself.

🍒 🐢 🍒

that's why tortoise exists.

because no one in this world deserves to go through anything like that alone.

not you. not anyone.

i know what it's like to feel like you have no one. to feel like your family wouldn't understand. to feel like your friends are busy with their own lives. to feel like you're carrying something heavy and there's nowhere to put it down.

but here's what florence taught me:

there are humans around us who will make us feel like we matter. sometimes they're strangers on a train. sometimes they're someone we meet for five minutes. sometimes they're people we've known forever but never really saw.

they exist. we exist. you exist.

you matter. you always have.

tortoise is for the moments when you need to hear that from someone. a human who just listens. no fixing. no judging. just:

"i hear you. i'm here. you're not alone."

i'm building this slowly. like a tortoise. because the things that last are the things that take time.

and if you're reading this and something in you feels seen —

thank you for being here. and thank you for being you.

🐢
deva
